Hi, my name is OnYou and yes, it's my real name.


OnYou in Korean means warm and soft, and it also has biblical context as 'OnYou' is an adjective that is only used in the Bible in Korea. So growing up, I always took pride in my name and since it is a very unique name in Korean as well, I got a compliment on my name pretty often.

But unfortunately, that was not the case when I started going to high school in Minnesota. High school kids being high school kids, they made a joke out of my name saying things such as "oh she's on you."

I was in a new country, a new school, using a new language. People making jokes out of my name instantly made me even more frustrated than I already was. So I decided to discuss it with my advisor, who brought me to talk to Mr. Simons, the Assistant Director of Student Life. We went to his office to discuss it and he says "You should get an English name."

So I took his advice. I tried for days to give myself an English name so can I can blend in with these American kids. But I just couldn't find an English name that I felt like myself in. So I just decided to go by OnYou, and I am so fucking glad that I didn't listen to Mr. Simons.

Back then, I didn't know how messed up this was for an "Assistant Director of Student Life" to tell a student with a non-western name to change the name that they lived with for their entire life to a western name. But I don't think he was aware of how a name can be a big part of people's identity for some individuals, including me.

So here we are.

My Name is a web-based interactive project that displays the archive of people's story regarding the relationship between their names and identities.

I hope this project to prevent adults from being individuals who can tell others to change their name—or identity—so easily. And I hope the project to inspire everyone to realize the value of their names and love their names.
