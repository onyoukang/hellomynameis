    This is how my name influences my identity: _______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________.

    One time when I was living in Australia, I changed my name to Sue because Australians couldn't say my name as it should be pronounced (So Ah; they would pronounce it Sue Ah)—I didn't really think much into it. When I moved to America, I was given the choice again to be called whatever I wanted to. I knew with having a space and capitals in my name it would cause a lot of confusion and difficulties in legal documents, but I still wanted to be called who I am. I thought, people can take that extra second to learn my name and I won't apologize for having a name that might be confusing. I guess that was when I first seriously considered how I wanted to be perceived, what it meant for my self brand—who I am. Name is actually such an important part of who I am; it is the first thing someone learns about me and sometimes even before meeting me. My web domain is what it is because of that reasoning as well—I am proud of who I am and where I come from and my name is the first look into all that
