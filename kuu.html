    This is how my name influences my identity: __________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________.

    I haven't met somebody else with the name "Kuu," so for me my name is mine alone (thus far). And to an extent, it means that my identification is mine alone (thus far). I didn't choose the name for that reason though, I didn't have a particular reason for the name "Kuu," I just wanted a new name. I honestly always disliked my real name, thinking that it felt weird when people called me by it. All my closest friends back at home who also called me by my real name at some point all think that "Kuu" is a better name for me too, so I chose correctly I think

    Here's a positive incident that my name played a significant role in my interaction with another person: _____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________.

    The first time I used the name "Kuu" was when I introduced myself to my freshman (and sophomore) year roommate. At the time, I had a short list of possible names I was consider-ing, and Kuu was actually never on it. I think I made it up on the spot, and I was so nervous. Because at the time, I was thinking "well if I say it and it feels wrong, I'm going to have to ...re-introduce myself with a different name to this person who I'm going to live with this year." Though nerve-wracking, it felt right when I said it, and I consider it a positive incident. Now my former roommate, she is still one of my closest friends, and I actually told her about this in retrospect and we both found it funny



    Here's a negative incident that my name played a significant role in my interaction with another person: ______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________.

    This isn't particularly negative, but because of the way it looks and sounds, a lot of people still make assumptions about the origin of my name. Though there may be subcons-cious choices involved in my choosing "Kuu," I dislike when people automatically ask where I am from just because it's an uncommon name in both English and Chinese
